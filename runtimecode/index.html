Executive Summary

This comprehensive proposal outlines two distinct and viable design options for enhancing the Data Center (DC) and Disaster Recovery (DR) capabilities for our Vietnam operations. The primary objectives are to enhance business continuity, minimize operational risk, and create a scalable architecture that supports future growth across our five Vietnam sites.

Our current infrastructure consists of VN-OFFICE-01 (main office) and VN-OFFICE-02 (secondary office) as primary operational hubs, along with three smaller branch sites. The existing connectivity includes dedicated internet links at both main offices and two private Point-to-Point (P2P) circuits connecting to our global corporate network.

The proposed solutions leverage a new commercial colocation data center (VN-DCH-01) to centralize critical infrastructure while optimizing the roles of existing sites. Both design options incorporate advanced technologies such as Software-Defined Wide Area Network (SD-WAN) for intelligent traffic management and enhanced security.

Design Option 1: Centralized Production DC with Active-Passive DR

High-Level Strategy

This design option proposes a centralized production data center (DC) in a new colocation facility (VN-DCH-01) with an active-passive disaster recovery (DR) strategy. In this model, VN-DCH-01 will serve as the primary operational hub, hosting all core servers, applications, and critical network equipment. VN-OFFICE-01 will transition to a role primarily focused on user access and local services, while VN-OFFICE-02 will serve as the passive DR site, maintaining a synchronized replica of critical data and systems from VN-DCH-01.

The core concept behind this strategy is to consolidate primary IT infrastructure into a purpose-built, secure, and resilient colocation data center, thereby enhancing operational efficiency, security, and scalability. The active-passive DR approach at VN-OFFICE-02 ensures business continuity by providing a warm standby environment that can take over operations in the event of a major disaster affecting VN-DCH-01.

Traffic Flow and Failover

During normal operations, all primary production traffic is directed to VN-DCH-01. Users at VN-OFFICE-01 and branch sites access applications through the SD-WAN overlay network, which provides secure and optimized paths. Data replication from VN-DCH-01 to VN-OFFICE-02 is continuous and asynchronous, ensuring minimal performance impact on the primary site.

In the event of a catastrophic failure at VN-DCH-01, the failover process involves detection, DR plan activation, DNS updates, system activation at VN-OFFICE-02, and network re-routing through SD-WAN and BGP convergence.

Recovery Time Objective (RTO)

2-4 hours - Time required for detection, activation, system startup, and traffic re-routing

Recovery Point Objective (RPO)

15-30 minutes - Potential data loss due to asynchronous replication interval

Advantages

  • Cost-Effectiveness: Lower operational costs compared to active-active setup
  • Simplicity: Less complex to design, implement, and manage
  • Clear Roles: Distinct responsibilities for each location
  • Reduced Data Contention: Single active site eliminates write conflicts
  • Established Technology: Mature and widely adopted strategy
  • Scalability: Centralized DC allows for better resource optimization

Disadvantages

  • Higher RTO: Failover process takes time to complete
  • Data Loss Potential: Asynchronous replication may result in data loss
  • Underutilized Resources: DR site resources are largely idle
  • Failback Complexity: Returning to primary site requires careful planning
  • Single Point of Failure: Dependency on successful DR activation
  • Testing Challenges: DR testing can be disruptive

Design Option 2: Geo-Clustered Active-Active DC

High-Level Strategy

This design option proposes a Geo-Clustered Active-Active Data Center solution, leveraging both the new colocation facility (VN-DCH-01) and VN-OFFICE-02 as active production sites. In this model, both data centers simultaneously host and serve applications and data, distributing the workload and providing immediate failover capabilities.

The core concept is to achieve maximum business continuity and disaster avoidance by eliminating single points of failure and ensuring both data centers are fully operational at all times. This approach requires advanced technologies such as Global Server Load Balancing (GSLB) for intelligent traffic distribution and Data Center Interconnect (DCI) for high-speed, low-latency communication between active sites.

Branch Sites Remote Access Basic network devices, SD-WAN edge SD-WAN to nearest active DC

Traffic Flow and Failover

In the active-active architecture, both VN-DCH-01 and VN-OFFICE-02 simultaneously serve production traffic. User traffic is intelligently routed to the nearest or most optimal active data center by GSLB. Bidirectional data replication between sites is continuous and synchronous or near-synchronous over the high-speed DCI.

Failover is largely automated and transparent to users. When one active DC fails, GSLB automatically redirects all traffic to the remaining active site within seconds, with no need to power on systems or recover data.

Recovery Time Objective (RTO)

Near-zero (seconds to minutes) - Automatic traffic redirection via GSLB

Recovery Point Objective (RPO)

Near-zero (seconds) - Synchronous data replication over DCI

Advantages

  • Near-Zero RTO: Instantaneous failover with minimal downtime
  • Near-Zero RPO: Synchronous replication eliminates data loss
  • Enhanced Performance: Load distribution improves user experience
  • Optimal Resource Utilization: Both DCs actively contribute to production
  • Disaster Avoidance: True disaster prevention rather than recovery
  • Simplified Testing: Less disruptive maintenance and testing

Disadvantages

  • Higher Cost: Significantly more expensive to implement and maintain
  • Increased Complexity: Complex design, implementation, and management
  • Data Consistency Challenges: Ensuring consistency across distributed sites
  • Application Compatibility: Not all applications support active-active deployment
  • Network Latency Sensitivity: Performance depends on inter-site connectivity
  • Operational Overhead: Requires highly skilled team and robust monitoring

Recommendation: Design Option 1

Based on the current infrastructure, objectives, and detailed analysis of both design options, I recommend Design Option 1: Centralized Production DC with Active-Passive DR for your Vietnam operations.

Justification

While Design Option 2 offers superior RTO and RPO with near-zero downtime and data loss, its complexity and significantly higher cost make it less suitable for the initial phase of enhancing business continuity for a company with an expanding presence in Vietnam. The recommendation emphasizes enhancing business continuity, minimizing risk, and creating a scalable architecture for future growth, while being open to renting rack space in a commercial colocation data center.

Key Reasons:

  • Cost-Effectiveness: More manageable initial investment and operational costs
  • Simplicity: Less complex to implement and manage, reducing operational overhead
  • Achieves Core Objectives: Significantly improves business continuity and resilience
  • Leverages Existing Infrastructure: Optimizes use of current assets
  • Foundation for Growth: Provides a solid base for future expansion to active-active if needed

Key Technologies:

  • SD-WAN: Enables intelligent traffic routing and enhanced security across all sites
  • Asynchronous Data Replication: Balances data currency with operational efficiency
  • Colocation Data Center: Provides enterprise-grade infrastructure and scalability

Conclusion

The recommended Centralized Production DC with Active-Passive DR solution provides a balanced and practical approach to enhancing business continuity for your Vietnam operations. This design offers significant improvements in resilience and disaster recovery capabilities while maintaining manageable costs and complexity.

The solution effectively addresses the primary objectives of enhancing business continuity, minimizing risk, and creating a scalable architecture for future growth. It also provides a solid foundation that can be evolved into a more advanced active-active configuration as the company's operations in Vietnam mature and critical application requirements evolve.

Implementation of this solution will result in a more resilient, secure, and scalable IT infrastructure that supports current business needs while providing the flexibility to adapt to future requirements.
